# The Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region Mediation Rules (2025 Edition)

## 1. Scope and Application (Rule 1 & Policy)
*   **Effective Date:** 6 February 2025.
*   **Policy Objective:** Implements the HKSAR Government's "Mediation First" policy by providing standard rules for mediation clauses incorporated into Government contracts, requiring mediation prior to arbitration or litigation.
*   **Application:** Applies to mediations conducted pursuant to mediation clauses in Government contracts or other non-contractual disputes involving the Government.

## 2. Commencement of Mediation & Response (Rule 4)
*   **Request for Mediation:** A party initiating mediation shall deliver a written Request for Mediation to the other party.
*   **Response Timeframe:** The responding party must reply in writing within **14 days** of receipt of the Request.
*   **Deemed Refusal:** Failure to respond within 14 days or refusal to agree on the minimum level of participation/mediator nomination shall be deemed a refusal to mediate.

## 3. Appointment of Mediator (Rule 5)
*   **Number of Mediators:** Unless otherwise agreed by the parties, there shall be a **sole mediator**.
*   **Appointment:** The mediator shall be appointed jointly by the parties or by an agreed appointing authority if parties fail to agree within specified timeframes.

## 4. Conduct and Duration of Mediation (Rule 6)
*   **Time Limit:** The mediator and the parties shall use their best endeavors to complete the mediation within **42 days** from the date of appointment of the mediator, unless extended by written agreement of the parties.

## 5. Representation and Authority (Rule 11)
*   **Minimum Participation:** Parties must provide reasonable cooperation and meet the required **minimum level of participation**.
*   **Authority to Settle:** Party representatives attending mediation sessions must possess **full authority to settle** the dispute (or have immediate access to such decision-makers).

## 6. Confidentiality and Without Prejudice Privilege (Rules 13, 14 & 15)
*   **Private and Confidential:** All mediation sessions, communications, documents prepared, and proposals made during the course of mediation are strictly confidential and without prejudice.
*   **Inadmissibility:** No mediation communication or document shall be admissible as evidence in subsequent arbitral or judicial proceedings, subject to standard statutory exceptions under the Mediation Ordinance (Cap. 620).
